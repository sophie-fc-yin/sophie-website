Every creator I talk to has the same blind spot: they read a handful of comments after publishing, reply to a few, and move on. Meanwhile, thousands of comments pile up containing the most valuable audience research they'll ever have access to — for free.

The questions people ask in your comments tell you exactly what content to make next. The frustrations they share tell you what problems to solve. The suggestions they offer tell you what features to build. And almost nobody is systematically looking at any of it.

This isn't because creators are lazy. It's because the volume is unmanageable without systems. You can't read 10,000 comments and spot patterns. But a pipeline that embeds, clusters, and summarizes them can — and it takes minutes, not weeks.

I built a system that does exactly this. It pulls comments from the YouTube API, generates semantic embeddings, clusters them by meaning, and runs an insight agent over the results. The output is a clean summary of your audience's top questions, recurring pain points, and content gaps.

The first time I ran it on a real channel, the creator's reaction was: "I had no idea my audience was asking for this." That single insight changed their next quarter of content strategy.

Your comment section isn't a vanity metric. It's a dataset. Start treating it like one.
